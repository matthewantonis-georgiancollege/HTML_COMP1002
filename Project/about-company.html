Our History

The Remarkable Journey of Nintendo (1889-1987) Founded in 1889 by Fusajiro Yamauchi, Nintendo has a rich and diverse history. What began as a humble playing card company in Kyoto, Japan, has transformed into an international gaming empire, beloved by millions of fans around the world. The following account of Nintendo's journey up until 1987 showcases the company's unwavering dedication to quality, innovation, and entertainment.


In its early days, Nintendo Koppai, as it was known then, produced handmade Hanafuda cards. These cards, adorned with intricate artwork, quickly gained popularity across Japan. By 1907, Nintendo had expanded its reach, partnering with the Japan Tobacco & Salt Corporation to distribute its cards nationally.


Throughout the years, Nintendo continued to refine its products and branch out into new markets. In 1953, they adopted modern Western-style playing cards, and by the 1960s, they began exploring various business ventures, including instant rice, taxi services, and even love hotels. However, these experiments were short-lived, as Nintendo soon returned to its core business: games and entertainment.


In 1966, Gunpei Yokoi, an assembly line worker, caught the eye of Hiroshi Yamauchi, Fusajiro Yamauchi's grandson and then-president of Nintendo. Yokoi's innovation, the Ultra Hand, was a simple extendable arm toy that became a massive success. This marked the beginning of Nintendo's foray into the world of electronic gaming.


Throughout the 1970s, Nintendo continued to make its mark in the gaming industry, producing popular light gun games like Wild Gunman and various arcade games such as EVR Race. In 1977, the company released its first home console, the Color TV-Game series, in collaboration with Mitsubishi Electronics.


The early 1980s were a pivotal time for Nintendo. In 1980, they introduced the Game & Watch series, a line of handheld electronic games designed by Gunpei Yokoi. These devices featured an LCD screen and a simple control scheme, paving the way for future handheld gaming devices.


In 1981, Shigeru Miyamoto, a young artist and game designer, created one of the most iconic video games in history: Donkey Kong. This game introduced the world to the now-legendary character Mario, a plucky Italian-American plumber with a red cap and blue overalls. Donkey Kong's immense success propelled Nintendo into the international gaming scene.


Nintendo continued to innovate, launching the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES) in Japan in 1983 and in North America in 1985. The NES revitalized the home video game market, which had been suffering from a crash in 1983. Bundled with the instant classic Super Mario Bros., the NES quickly became a household staple, selling millions of units and solidifying Nintendo's place in the gaming industry.


In 1986, the release of The Legend of Zelda revolutionized the action-adventure genre. Designed by Shigeru Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka, this game introduced non-linear gameplay and a rich, immersive world for players to explore. The Legend of Zelda quickly became a critical and commercial success, further establishing Nintendo's reputation for creating captivating and engaging gaming experiences.


By 1987, Nintendo had truly become a global phenomenon. With a lineup of unforgettable games and groundbreaking consoles, the company had captured the hearts of gamers worldwide. From its humble beginnings as a playing card manufacturer to its meteoric rise as a gaming juggernaut, Nintendo's story is a testament to the power of creativity, perseverance, and a genuine passion for entertainment.


Today, Nintendo's legacy lives on as a trusted brand that consistently delivers quality products and experiences to consumers. As we look back on the company's history, from 1889 to 1987, we can appreciate the innovation and determination that fueled Nintendo's growth. By remaining dedicated to producing captivating games and pushing the boundaries of technology, Nintendo has earned its place as a beloved and enduring presence in the world of entertainment.
